# FE-012C Repeatability Interpretation Rules

## Core Rules

- Do not treat repeatability as proof.
- Do not treat one drift as falsification.
- Look for repeated drift patterns.

## Interpretation Focus

- Compare current GPT vs prior GPT.
- Compare current Claude vs prior Claude.
- Compare current Gemini vs prior Gemini.
- Compare current cross-model agreement.
- Compare prior cross-model agreement.

## Caution

Agreement may reflect a stable instrument, a constrained vocabulary, recognizable artifact structure, or shared model priors.

Disagreement may reflect drift, ambiguity in the packet, ambiguity in the vocabulary, model-specific behavior, or instability in the measurement instrument.

Interpretation should distinguish these possibilities where evidence permits.
