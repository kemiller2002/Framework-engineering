# FE-011A Pilot Summary

Date: 2026-06-28

Status: Internal pilot run completed.

## Comparative Outcome

- SWOT:
  Output 1 was judged stronger overall than Output 2, mainly because it improved traceability and actionability without introducing severe clarity loss.
- Five Whys:
  Output 2 was judged stronger overall than Output 1 because it preserved uncertainty, considered alternative hypotheses, and tied claims back to available evidence.
- OODA:
  Output 2 was judged stronger overall than Output 1 because it made assumptions and reassessment triggers explicit, although it was more complex.

## Cross-Framework Pattern

- The more structured outputs generally improved evidence use and traceability.
- Added structure also increased verbosity and process overhead.
- The strongest gains appeared where the original packet under-specified uncertainty handling and verification.
- The pilot does not cleanly separate Framework Engineering effects from generic scaffolding effects.

## Interpretation

- The pilot produced directional evidence that structured redesigns can improve traceability and evaluation readiness.
- The pilot also reinforced the methodological concern documented in `methodology-review.md`: the redesigned packets may benefit from added structure even if Framework Engineering itself is not the causal factor.
- These results justify further refinement and a more rigorous follow-on study, not a claim of proof.
