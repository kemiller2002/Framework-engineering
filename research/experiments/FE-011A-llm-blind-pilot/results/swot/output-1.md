# SWOT Output 1

## Scope

Assess the restaurant chain's near-term decision context using only the facts in the scenario, with emphasis on declining visits, competitive pressure, and operational instability.

## Observations

- The chain still has positive online reviews.
- The chain retains a strong local reputation in several neighborhoods.
- Customer visits are declining.
- Competitors have introduced delivery-first offers and loyalty programs.
- The chain has limited capital for major expansion or renovation.
- Staffing instability is causing schedule disruption and service inconsistency.
- Food costs are rising.

## SWOT Classification

### Strengths

- Positive online reviews
- Strong local reputation in several neighborhoods

### Weaknesses

- Declining visits
- Staffing instability
- Service inconsistency from schedule disruptions
- Limited capital for major expansion or renovation
- Exposure to rising food costs

### Opportunities

- Respond to competitor delivery-first pressure with a lower-capital delivery response such as partnerships or selective rollout
- Use the existing local reputation as a retention asset
- Consider a loyalty approach because competitors are using loyalty programs to strengthen repeat traffic

### Threats

- Competitors are improving convenience and retention through delivery and loyalty programs
- Rising food costs can compress margins further
- Continued service inconsistency may erode the value of current positive reviews and local reputation

## Evidence / Confidence

- Positive reviews and local reputation as strengths: high
- Staffing instability and rising food costs as active weaknesses: high
- Competitor delivery and loyalty programs as external threats: high
- Delivery or loyalty response as a practical opportunity: medium
- Any major capital-intensive response: low, because the scenario explicitly states limited capital

## Priorities

1. Stabilize service consistency enough to protect existing reputation.
2. Decide whether to counter the convenience gap with a low-capital delivery or loyalty response.
3. Manage food-cost exposure before margin pressure compounds declining visits.

## Recommended Actions

- Address staffing instability first because service inconsistency can damage both current visits and future reputation.
- Evaluate a low-capital response to the competitor convenience gap rather than assuming a large expansion program is feasible.
- Use the chain's local reputation as the basis for targeted retention efforts instead of relying on reputation alone to reverse the decline in visits.
