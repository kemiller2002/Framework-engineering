# FE-012A Internal Run Summary

Date: 2026-06-28

Status: Internal single-extractor run completed.

## Execution Scope

- 20 non-EDF, non-Clarity artifacts processed.
- 1 extractor pass: `EX-01`
- 1 reconstruction analyst pass: `RA-01`
- Same model family used for extraction and reconstruction.

## Primary Observations

- The seed vocabulary covered most extraction cases without requiring new accepted primitives.
- Two candidate primitives emerged under pressure from multiple artifacts:
  - `Prioritize`
  - `Compare`
- Vocabulary growth slowed materially after the middle of the artifact set.
- Diagnostic, cyclic, and intervention-oriented artifacts reconstructed more cleanly than representational or hierarchy-oriented artifacts.

## Reconstruction Pattern

- Strong: 9
- Moderate: 8
- Weak: 3
- Failed: 0
- Recognizable: 17 of 20

Artifacts with weakest reconstruction:

- Bloom's Taxonomy
- Business Model Canvas
- Hoshin Kanri

## Interpretation

- The run provides provisional support for a finite primitive vocabulary with transfer across multiple domains.
- The support is not decisive because no independent extractor agreement was measured.
- The result is more convincing for procedural reasoning artifacts than for representational or classificatory artifacts.
- The candidate primitives suggest the current seed set may still be compressing distinct functions that matter in practice.

## Methodological Limits

- This was a same-model internal run, not an independent blinded multi-analyst study.
- The extraction and reconstruction passes were performed with prior awareness of Framework Engineering research goals.
- Recognizability judgments remain partly subjective.

## Provisional Conclusion

FE-012A did not collapse immediately into artifact-specific vocabulary.

That is useful evidence for continued study.

It is not evidence that the primitive set is complete, final, or independently validated.
