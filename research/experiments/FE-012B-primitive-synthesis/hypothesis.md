# FE-012B Hypothesis

## Research Question

Can the provisional primitive reasoning vocabulary generate coherent procedural reasoning frameworks for previously unseen problems?

## Primary Hypothesis

A finite primitive reasoning vocabulary possesses sufficient expressive power to synthesize coherent procedural reasoning frameworks across multiple domains.

## Success Conditions

- Diverse procedural frameworks emerge from the same primitive vocabulary.
- Reviewers judge synthesized frameworks coherent.
- Primitive vocabulary remains stable.
- Missing primitive requests become rare.

## Failure Conditions

- Synthesis repeatedly requires new primitives.
- Reviewers identify major reasoning gaps.
- Frameworks become artifact-specific.
- Primitive composition becomes inconsistent.

## Constraint

Use the frozen FE-012A primitive vocabulary.

Do not introduce new primitives during synthesis.

If synthesis appears impossible, document the missing primitive instead of inventing one.
