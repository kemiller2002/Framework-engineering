# FE-012C Primitive Definitions

These definitions are provisional.

They do not imply that the primitive list is complete.

## Bound

Purpose:
Define the scope, boundary, or target of the reasoning task.

Allowed interpretation:
Framing what is in scope before proceeding.

Examples:
Define the problem. Set the objective. Bound the system.

Non-examples:
Rank options. Execute a change.

Notes:
Often appears early but may be implicit in some artifacts.

## Observe

Purpose:
Acquire information about the situation or system.

Allowed interpretation:
Looking, gathering, inspecting, sensing, or recording relevant facts.

Examples:
Observe the condition. Gather signals. Review findings.

Non-examples:
Interpret why the facts matter. Choose an option.

Notes:
Observation should remain distinct from meaning assignment when possible.

## Classify

Purpose:
Organize information into categories or types.

Allowed interpretation:
Sort or place items into a defined structure.

Examples:
Group factors. Place a case into a condition type.

Non-examples:
Judge quality. Act on the system.

Notes:
Strongly associated with categorical artifacts.

## Interpret

Purpose:
Assign meaning to observations or context.

Allowed interpretation:
Explain what observations imply without necessarily building a full causal account.

Examples:
Interpret context. Read implications. Abstract meaning.

Non-examples:
Simply collect facts. Commit to an intervention.

Notes:
May overlap with `Explain` in ambiguous cases.

## Explain

Purpose:
Develop causal or explanatory understanding.

Allowed interpretation:
Trace why something happened or how elements relate causally.

Examples:
Explain the cause. Build a causal chain.

Non-examples:
Sort categories. Merely compare options.

Notes:
Most common in diagnostic and analytical artifacts.

## Generate

Purpose:
Produce options, hypotheses, candidate explanations, or alternatives.

Allowed interpretation:
Create a set of possible answers or actions.

Examples:
Generate options. Propose hypotheses.

Non-examples:
Choose the best option. Verify a result.

Notes:
Can be sparse in tightly constrained procedural artifacts.

## Evaluate

Purpose:
Judge something against criteria, goals, or evidence.

Allowed interpretation:
Assess quality, fit, severity, or adequacy.

Examples:
Evaluate performance. Assess fit. Judge severity.

Non-examples:
Only compare alternatives without judging them. Execute an intervention.

Notes:
May compress too much if `Compare` and `Prioritize` are genuinely distinct.

## Compare

Purpose:
Contrast alternatives or states to identify relative differences.

Allowed interpretation:
Side-by-side comparison.

Examples:
Compare options. Contrast alternatives.

Non-examples:
Generic evaluation without explicit relative contrast.

Notes:
Currently retained as a provisional primitive because some artifacts depend heavily on explicit contrast.

## Prioritize

Purpose:
Order issues, options, or actions by relative importance or urgency.

Allowed interpretation:
Ranking under constraint.

Examples:
Prioritize tasks. Rank interventions.

Non-examples:
Only evaluate without ordering. Simply communicate a result.

Notes:
Important in constrained-allocation and coordination problems.

## Decide

Purpose:
Commit to an option or conclusion.

Allowed interpretation:
Select a course of action or make a judgment.

Examples:
Choose an option. Commit to a response.

Non-examples:
Generate possibilities. Verify evidence.

Notes:
Decision may be explicit or implied by commitment.

## Act

Purpose:
Change the system or initiate an intervention.

Allowed interpretation:
Execute a chosen move.

Examples:
Implement the action. Carry out the response.

Non-examples:
Evaluate a scenario. Review outcomes afterward.

Notes:
Prototype-style moves may sometimes blur action and testing.

## Verify

Purpose:
Test whether a claim, conclusion, or result holds.

Allowed interpretation:
Check, confirm, or test the current judgment.

Examples:
Verify the result. Test the hypothesis.

Non-examples:
Simply observe without checking a claim. Reflect on lessons.

Notes:
Often a key closure control.

## Reflect

Purpose:
Extract lessons or update understanding from experience.

Allowed interpretation:
Review outcomes for learning.

Examples:
Reflect on what happened. Capture lessons learned.

Non-examples:
Immediate intervention. Initial observation.

Notes:
May be underused in forward-only procedural artifacts.

## Reassess

Purpose:
Reopen a prior judgment when new evidence or conditions appear.

Allowed interpretation:
Revise or revisit a prior conclusion.

Examples:
Reassess the plan. Revisit the classification.

Non-examples:
Initial evaluation without revision pressure.

Notes:
Important in dynamic or iterative contexts.

## Communicate

Purpose:
Transmit reasoning, decisions, or relevant information to others.

Allowed interpretation:
Inform, align, or report.

Examples:
Communicate the plan. Share the rationale.

Non-examples:
Internally evaluate. Execute the intervention itself.

Notes:
Especially important in coordination-heavy artifacts.

## Allocate

Purpose:
Assign resources, effort, or assets to tasks or targets.

Allowed interpretation:
Distribute constrained capacity.

Examples:
Allocate resources. Assign effort.

Non-examples:
Generic prioritization without assignment.

Notes:
Included as a candidate execution-heavy primitive, not a settled one.

## Coordinate

Purpose:
Align actions across multiple actors, units, or systems.

Allowed interpretation:
Maintain joint action coherence.

Examples:
Coordinate teams. Align moving parts.

Non-examples:
Single-agent execution. Pure evaluation.

Notes:
Included because coordination pressure appeared in FE-012B.

## Schedule

Purpose:
Place actions in time or sequence windows.

Allowed interpretation:
Timing or temporal planning.

Examples:
Schedule work. Set timing.

Non-examples:
Generic prioritization without time placement.

Notes:
Still a hypothesis-level execution primitive.

## Sequence

Purpose:
Order actions into a necessary progression.

Allowed interpretation:
Specify step order where order matters materially.

Examples:
Sequence steps. Order phases.

Non-examples:
Merely list tasks without dependency.

Notes:
Still a hypothesis-level execution primitive.

## Synchronize

Purpose:
Keep parallel actions aligned in time or dependency state.

Allowed interpretation:
Concurrency alignment.

Examples:
Synchronize activities. Keep operations aligned.

Non-examples:
Simple communication or prioritization alone.

Notes:
Still a hypothesis-level execution primitive.
