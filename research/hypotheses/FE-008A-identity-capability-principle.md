# FE-008A

## Identity-Capability Principle

Status: Hypothesis

## Research Question

Can knowledge artifacts be described more accurately by separating identity from capabilities?

## Hypothesis

A knowledge artifact's identity is determined by its dominant purpose.

A knowledge artifact's capabilities describe the functions it can perform.

Identity and capabilities are related but independent properties.

Separating them should improve classification consistency and reduce reviewer disagreement.

## Predictions

- Reviewers will agree more frequently on identity than under the previous primary/secondary artifact model.
- Capabilities will exhibit natural overlap across artifact identities.
- Framework Engineering Cube will become simpler.
- Boundary cases such as Scrum, Kanban, MITRE ATT&CK, BPMN, TOGAF, and CMMI will become easier to classify.

## Evidence

Derived from FE-008 Pilot Batch 001 and Batch 002.

## Success Criteria

- Improved reviewer agreement.
- Reduced ambiguity.
- Cleaner operational definitions.

## Failure Criteria

- Capabilities become indistinguishable from identity.
- Reviewer disagreement remains unchanged.
