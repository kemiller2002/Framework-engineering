# Evidence To Theory Map

Purpose:
Show how each major experiment feeds into theory statements, predictions, and future work.

## FE-008

Observed findings

- Identity-Capability separation reduced overlap pressure in artifact characterization.
- Artifact characterization appears more stable when observable properties are recorded before classification.

Theory statements supported

- Identity-Capability separation improves characterization.
- Framework Engineering currently functions as a characterization methodology.

Predictions generated

- Independent reviewers should converge more reliably when identity and capability are separated.
- Artifact characterization should remain more auditable than single-label classification.

Future experiments required

- Larger blind reviewer comparison
- Comparison against alternative characterization schemes

## FE-011A

Observed findings

- Structured redesigns improved traceability and evidence handling.
- Added structure also increased complexity and overhead.
- The gains may be partly explained by structure in general rather than by Framework Engineering specifically.

Theory statements supported

- Structured redesign improves traceability.
- Framework Engineering currently functions as a redesign methodology.
- Several provisional design heuristics may improve reasoning quality.

Predictions generated

- Frameworks separating observation, interpretation, uncertainty, and verification will often produce more auditable outputs.
- Simplicity and usefulness may degrade if added structure exceeds the actual problem need.

Future experiments required

- Independent evaluator runs
- Controls using non-FE structured baselines
- Human participant validation

## FE-012A

Observed findings

- A small primitive vocabulary reconstructed many reasoning-heavy artifacts with moderate or strong recognizability.
- Vocabulary growth slowed after the middle of the artifact set.
- Representational and hierarchy-oriented artifacts reconstructed less cleanly.

Theory statements supported

- Procedural reasoning may be represented by a finite primitive vocabulary.
- Reasoning-heavy artifacts may compress more cleanly into a primitive grammar than other artifact types.

Predictions generated

- Independent extractors will converge more strongly on reasoning-heavy artifacts than on coordination-heavy artifacts.
- Primitive stabilization will weaken outside familiar procedural families if the current vocabulary is incomplete.

Future experiments required

- Independent extractor comparison
- Merge-disagreement audit
- Expansion into coordination-heavy artifact sets

## FE-012B

Observed findings

- The frozen primitive vocabulary synthesized coherent procedural structures in many novelty problems.
- Coordination-heavy and phased-maintenance problems created missing primitive pressure.
- `Compare` and `Prioritize` remained structurally important rather than collapsing away.

Theory statements supported

- Primitive vocabulary can synthesize coherent procedural structures.
- Reasoning grammar and execution grammar may be distinct.
- Framework Engineering is currently stronger at characterization and redesign than at full framework generation.

Predictions generated

- Coordination-heavy artifacts will require additional execution primitives more often than diagnostic artifacts.
- A generic reasoning grammar may transfer further than a coordination grammar.

Future experiments required

- Independent synthesis and review
- Direct tests of candidate execution primitives
- Reasoning-heavy versus coordination-heavy comparison studies

## Traceability Summary

FE-008 contributes most strongly to characterization theory.

FE-011A contributes most strongly to redesign and heuristic theory.

FE-012A contributes most strongly to reconstruction and primitive vocabulary theory.

FE-012B contributes most strongly to synthesis limits and grammar-splitting hypotheses.
