# Competing Explanations

Purpose:
Record the strongest non-FE explanations for current findings so theory claims remain challengeable.

## 1. Identity-Capability separation is an artifact of instrument structure

Description

The improved characterization may come from forcing analysts to answer clearer questions, not from a real structural distinction between identity and capability.

Supporting arguments

- Structured instruments often increase consistency even when the underlying categories are weak.
- FE-008 evidence is still pilot-scale.

Experiments capable of distinguishing between explanations

- Compare identity-capability characterization against equally structured alternative instruments.
- Test whether agreement remains higher when reviewers are blinded to Framework Engineering terminology.

## 2. Primitive stabilization results from prompt engineering

Description

The apparent stabilization in FE-012A may reflect how the extractor was prompted rather than a real bounded vocabulary.

Supporting arguments

- Same-model extraction can create artificial convergence.
- Early seed vocabulary may anchor later normalization choices.

Experiments capable of distinguishing between explanations

- Use independent extractors with varied prompts and separate merge audits.
- Compare stabilization with and without seed-vocabulary exposure.

## 3. Framework redesign improvements arise from generic structured thinking

Description

FE-011A improvements may come from generic structured prompts rather than from Framework Engineering itself.

Supporting arguments

- More sections often improve traceability regardless of theory.
- FE-011A already documented complexity costs from added structure.

Experiments capable of distinguishing between explanations

- Compare FE-based redesigns against non-FE structured baselines matched for complexity.
- Measure whether FE-specific separations outperform generic checklists.

## 4. Reasoning and execution grammars are not separate

Description

The observed split may be an artifact of an incomplete vocabulary rather than evidence for two grammar families.

Supporting arguments

- Coordination-heavy problems may only require a few more temporal primitives.
- The current distinction is inferred from limited internal runs.

Experiments capable of distinguishing between explanations

- Test whether adding a few temporal primitives allows one unified grammar to handle both artifact families.
- Compare model fit of a single expanded grammar versus a separated-grammar account.

## 5. Synchronization is a specialized reasoning primitive, not a new grammar signal

Description

Terms like `Synchronize`, `Schedule`, or `Sequence` may not indicate a distinct execution grammar. They may simply be subtypes of planning or decision moves.

Supporting arguments

- Coordination pressure may still be reducible to refined decision and prioritization logic.
- FE-012B did not independently validate candidate execution primitives.

Experiments capable of distinguishing between explanations

- Attempt blind extraction on coordination-heavy artifacts with forced reduction into the existing reasoning set.
- Compare reviewer coherence with and without dedicated coordination primitives.

## 6. Framework Engineering overlaps entirely with existing process engineering

Description

Framework Engineering may not represent a distinct methodology. It may be a re-description of established systems analysis, process design, and structured reasoning practices.

Supporting arguments

- Many observed gains resemble known benefits from process decomposition and traceability discipline.
- Current evidence is stronger for analysis and redesign than for unique generation capability.

Experiments capable of distinguishing between explanations

- Compare FE outputs against existing process-engineering methods under matched conditions.
- Test whether FE predicts or improves structures that standard process methods miss.
