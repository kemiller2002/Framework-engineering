# Grammar Hypotheses v0.1

Status: Hypotheses only

These are not accepted primitives.

## Purpose

Record the current grammar hypotheses emerging from FE-012A and FE-012B without treating them as settled architecture.

## Hypothesis 1

Procedural reasoning grammar and execution or coordination grammar may be distinct.

Rationale:

FE-012A reconstructed many reasoning-heavy artifacts without major pressure for coordination primitives.

FE-012B produced missing-primitive pressure in coordination-heavy synthesis problems.

## Hypothesis 2

Reasoning-heavy artifacts may rely primarily on primitives such as:

- Bound
- Observe
- Interpret
- Explain
- Generate
- Evaluate
- Decide
- Verify
- Reflect
- Reassess

These appear repeatedly in diagnostic, analytical, and evaluative flows.

## Hypothesis 3

Execution-heavy artifacts may require additional primitives such as:

- Synchronize
- Schedule
- Sequence
- Allocate
- Coordinate

These are hypotheses, not accepted primitives.

They are pressure terms inferred from FE-012B gaps, not validated additions.

## Hypothesis 4

Some primitives may function as bridge primitives across both grammars.

Possible bridge candidates:

- Bound
- Observe
- Decide
- Communicate
- Reassess

## Current Weaknesses

- The current evidence comes from internal runs rather than independent multi-analyst testing.
- The candidate execution primitives have not been independently extracted from blinded artifacts.
- The boundary between `Evaluate`, `Compare`, and `Prioritize` remains unsettled.

## Next Tests

- Blind extraction focused on coordination-heavy artifacts
- Independent synthesis with and without candidate execution primitives
- Direct comparison of reasoning-heavy and execution-heavy artifact families
