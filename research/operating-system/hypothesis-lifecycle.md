# Hypothesis Lifecycle

## 1. Proposed

Meaning:
A candidate hypothesis has been stated but not yet incorporated into active evidence planning.

Entry Criteria:
- clear statement exists
- rationale exists

Exit Criteria:
- hypothesis is accepted into active tracking or rejected

Required Evidence:
- conceptual rationale only

## 2. Active

Meaning:
The hypothesis is being tracked and has at least one planned or running test.

Entry Criteria:
- kill condition defined
- next test defined
- registry entry exists

Exit Criteria:
- hypothesis is strengthened, weakened, revised, suspended, or retired

Required Evidence:
- at least one meaningful test planned

## 3. Strengthened

Meaning:
Recent evidence increased confidence without fully validating the hypothesis.

Entry Criteria:
- prediction survived a meaningful test

Exit Criteria:
- hypothesis returns to active tracking, is further strengthened, or is weakened

Required Evidence:
- observed support from a meaningful test

## 4. Weakened

Meaning:
Recent evidence reduced confidence or strengthened competing explanations.

Entry Criteria:
- failed prediction
- elevated competing explanation
- upstream dependency weakened

Exit Criteria:
- hypothesis is revised, suspended, retired, or narrowed and retested

Required Evidence:
- concrete contradictory or destabilizing evidence

Rule:
A weakened hypothesis should be narrowed before being expanded.

## 5. Revised

Meaning:
The hypothesis has been materially rewritten to fit the evidence better.

Entry Criteria:
- previous wording no longer matches observations well

Exit Criteria:
- revised hypothesis becomes active or is suspended

Required Evidence:
- explicit rationale for revision

## 6. Suspended

Meaning:
The hypothesis is paused because current evidence or infrastructure is insufficient.

Entry Criteria:
- unresolved ambiguity persists
- dependency failure blocks interpretation
- abstraction value is unproven

Exit Criteria:
- new evidence or revised instrument justifies reactivation

Required Evidence:
- documented reason for suspension

## 7. Retired

Meaning:
The hypothesis should no longer guide research decisions.

Entry Criteria:
- kill condition met repeatedly
- repeated failures without compensating evidence

Exit Criteria:
- none, unless a new hypothesis is proposed separately

Required Evidence:
- explicit retirement rationale
