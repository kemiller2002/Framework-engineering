# Uncertainty to Action Flow

1. Mission establishes why the reasoning matters.
2. Objective defines what the actor is trying to accomplish.
3. Question identifies uncertainty.
4. Beliefs represent possible answers.
5. Evidence changes belief confidence.
6. Kill conditions define when beliefs should be revised or abandoned.
7. Decisions occur when confidence and context justify commitment.
8. Commitments transfer into EDF.
9. EDF manages execution, coordination, verification, and reassessment.

## Distinction

Clarity:
Manages uncertainty, beliefs, confidence, and decisions.

EDF:
Manages commitments, execution, coordination, verification, and delivery.

Framework Engineering:
Studies the structure of procedural reasoning and provides research findings that may inform both Clarity and EDF.
