# ECR-000003 Hypothesis Review Board

## Scientifically Tightened Draft Decisions

This document records proposed Review Board decisions following
ECR-000003. The emphasis is on evidence-supported conclusions, explicit
alternative explanations, and operational kill conditions. These are
**proposed** decisions pending human approval.

# H002 -- Representation Independence

## Proposed Decision

**Retain**

### Board Interpretation

ECR-000003 provides evidence consistent with the idea that procedural
recovery is more stable than surface representation. However, the
evidence does not yet isolate representation effects from semantic
familiarity.

### Confidence

**Moderate increase**

### What Would Falsify or Materially Weaken H002?

-   Equivalent procedures consistently lose backbone agreement after
    representation changes alone.
-   Representation format predicts procedural recovery better than
    underlying procedure.
-   Human reviewers fail to recover common structure across equivalent
    representations.

### Highest-Information Next Experiment

Create multiple radically different representations of the same
procedure while holding semantics constant.

# H003 -- Framework Composition

## Proposed Decision

**Retain**

### Board Interpretation

Repeated emergence of similar primitive compositions supports the
hypothesis, but ECR-000003 does not yet demonstrate that compositions
are predictable or unique.

### Confidence

**Slight increase**

### Split Recommendation

Consider separating: 1. Primitive compositions exist. 2. Primitive
compositions are predictable.

### Material Weakening Criteria

-   Primitive decompositions become arbitrarily inconsistent across
    equivalent artifacts.
-   No stable composition patterns emerge under replication.

### Highest-Information Next Experiment

Attempt prediction of primitive composition for previously unseen
frameworks.

# H013 -- Recognition Bias

## Proposed Decision

**Retain and Split**

### Board Interpretation

Recognition appears to influence decomposition depth and elaboration,
but ECR-000003 does not demonstrate a single recognition phenomenon.
Multiple mechanisms likely exist.

### Confidence

**Moderate increase for the overall concept**

### Proposed Child Hypotheses

-   Lexical Recognition Bias
-   Structural Recognition Influence
-   Procedural Elaboration Bias

### Material Weakening Criteria

-   Recognition cues produce no measurable change after controlling for
    procedural structure.
-   Structural recovery remains unchanged regardless of recognition
    manipulation.

### Highest-Information Next Experiment

Introduce misleading labels while preserving identical procedural
topology.

# H015 -- Measurement Instrument Reliability

## Proposed Decision

**Retain**

### Board Interpretation

The strongest-supported hypothesis following ECR-000003. Evidence
demonstrates improved reproducibility and governance of the measurement
process rather than validation of Framework Engineering itself.

### Confidence

**Moderate increase**

### Material Weakening Criteria

-   Certified datasets produce different measurements when rerun.
-   Independent operators cannot reproduce published measurements.
-   Audit trails fail to explain reported results.

### Highest-Information Next Experiment

Independent external replication using frozen comparator and certified
datasets.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# H016 -- Structural Recognition

## Proposed Decision

**Retain**

### Board Interpretation

Evidence is consistent with structural recovery, but alternative
explanations remain plausible. The phenomenon has been observed but not
yet isolated.

### Confidence

**Slight increase**

### Material Weakening Criteria

-   Topology-preserving transformations consistently destroy recovered
    backbone.
-   After controlling semantic familiarity, structural agreement
    disappears.
-   Humans consistently recover incompatible structures under identical
    conditions.

### Highest-Information Next Experiment

Negative controls with misleading semantics but preserved procedural
topology.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# H017 -- Procedural Compression

## Proposed Decision

**Retain**

### Board Interpretation

Evidence suggests models compress procedures into higher-level
abstractions while preserving recognizable backbone.

### Confidence

**Moderate increase**

### Material Weakening Criteria

-   Compression consistently destroys procedural identity.
-   Compression behavior proves indistinguishable from generic
    summarization under controlled testing.

### Highest-Information Next Experiment

Controlled abstraction experiments with predefined compression targets.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# H018 -- Procedural Isomorphism

## Proposed Decision

**Retain**

### Board Interpretation

Evidence is consistent with shared procedural organization across
domains, but stronger isolation from domain semantics is required.

### Confidence

**Slight increase**

### Material Weakening Criteria

-   Isomorphic procedures consistently fail to produce comparable
    procedural backbones.
-   Domain vocabulary predicts procedural organization better than
    topology.

### Highest-Information Next Experiment

Construct unrelated domains sharing identical procedural graphs.

## Scientific Claims Guidance

The board concludes that ECR-000003 provides **evidence consistent
with** qualitative strengthening of multiple hypotheses. It does **not**
validate Framework Engineering as an engineering discipline, nor does it
eliminate competing explanations. The appropriate conclusion is that the
research program has become more mature and more testable, with the next
phase focused on discrimination between competing explanations rather
than accumulating additional observational evidence.
