# 4. Scientific Claims Review

## Claims Supported

-   Procedural backbone appears more stable than surface representation.
-   Comparator governance improved measurement reproducibility.
-   Multiple hypotheses received qualitative support.
-   Representation changes do not necessarily eliminate procedural
    recovery.
-   The research operating system successfully governed evidence from
    collection through review.

## Claims NOT Supported

-   Framework Engineering has been validated.
-   Structural recognition has been proven.
-   Primitive grammar is universal.
-   Framework Engineering is established as an engineering discipline.
-   Alternative explanations have been eliminated.

**Board Guidance:** Use language such as *"evidence is consistent with"*
rather than *"proves"* or *"demonstrates conclusively."*

# 6. Methodological Lessons

1.  Freeze measurement instruments before interpretation.
2.  Normalize datasets before comparison.
3.  Separate measurement from explainability.
4.  Use operational kill conditions.
5.  Distinguish evidence review from theory revision.
6.  Record confidence changes explicitly.
7.  Preserve alternative explanations.
8.  Prefer conservative scientific claims.

# Closing Statement

The Review Board concludes that ECR-000003 is suitable for closure. The
accepted evidence qualitatively strengthens several active hypotheses
while preserving important uncertainty and alternative explanations. The
Board recommends advancing to a Theory Review Board before initiating
any new evidence collection. The next research phase should prioritize
experiments that discriminate among competing explanations rather than
simply accumulating additional supportive observations.
