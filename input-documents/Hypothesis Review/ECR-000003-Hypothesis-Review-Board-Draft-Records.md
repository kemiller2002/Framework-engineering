# Remaining Hypothesis Review Records (Draft)

These draft records continue the same review-board format and evidence
standard established for H015 and H016. They are intended as proposed
decisions pending human approval.

## H002 -- Representation Independence

-   **Recommendation:** Retain
-   **Evidence Direction:** Supports provisionally
-   **Evidence Strength:** Moderate
-   **Confidence:** Increase qualitatively
-   **Supporting Evidence:** Representation-sensitive experiments
    preserved substantial procedural backbone; cross-representation
    recovery exceeded lexical agreement; structural organization
    remained more stable than wording.
-   **Challenges:** Constraint agreement remains inconsistent; human
    baseline unavailable; structural and semantic effects are not fully
    isolated.
-   **Alternative Explanations:** Semantic familiarity; comparator
    ontology bias.
-   **Recommended Kill Condition:** Substantially weaken if equivalent
    procedures consistently lose structural agreement solely because
    representation changes.
-   **Next Experiment:** Increase representation diversity while holding
    procedural content constant.

## H003 -- Framework Composition

-   **Recommendation:** Retain
-   **Evidence Direction:** Supports with unresolved scope questions
-   **Evidence Strength:** Weak to Moderate
-   **Confidence:** Slight increase
-   **Supporting Evidence:** Primitive compositions appeared repeatedly;
    similar procedural organizations emerged despite differing
    representations.
-   **Challenges:** Composition boundaries remain subjective; primitive
    decompositions differ across providers.
-   **Alternative Explanations:** Shared reasoning heuristics.
-   **Split Recommendation:** Consider separating composition existence
    from composition predictability.
-   **Next Experiment:** Test intentionally novel framework
    compositions.

## H013 -- Recognition Bias

-   **Recommendation:** Retain and Consider Split
-   **Evidence Direction:** Mixed but informative
-   **Evidence Strength:** Moderate
-   **Confidence:** Increase for overall concept; defer stronger claims
    until split.
-   **Supporting Evidence:** Recognition appears to influence
    decomposition depth; similar backbones coexisted with differing
    elaboration.
-   **Challenges:** Structural recovery persisted despite recognition
    changes; recognition not isolated from semantic familiarity.
-   **Proposed Split:** Lexical Recognition; Structural Recognition
    Influence; Procedural Elaboration Bias.
-   **Next Experiment:** Negative controls preserving structure while
    misleading recognition.

## H017 -- Procedural Compression

## H018 -- Procedural Isomorphism

-   **Recommendation:** Retain
-   **Evidence Direction:** Supports provisionally
-   **Evidence Strength:** Moderate
-   **Confidence:** Slight increase
-   **Supporting Evidence:** Similar procedural organization emerged
    across distinct domains; primitive roles more stable than
    terminology.
-   **Challenges:** Domain effects not fully isolated; human baseline

-   **Alternative Explanations:** Shared semantic templates.
-   **Recommended Kill Condition:** Weaken if topology-preserving
